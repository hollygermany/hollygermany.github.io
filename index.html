Holly – Biography

My name is Holly, and I am thirty years old. I am a well-rounded scholar with a wide range of interests. I began playing the piano when I was four and took up the violin at the age of six. I was once able to perform works such as Tchaikovsky’s Piano Concerto, and some of my favourite piano pieces to play included Chopin’s Scherzo No. 3, Rachmaninoff’s Op. 16 No. 4, and Scriabin’s Op. 8 No. 12. I particularly enjoy playing chamber music.

I am also an accomplished musician who has composed sonatas, concerti, operas, and fugues. From Year 2 to Year 6, I participated in Mathematics Olympiad programmes. I studied ballet for five years during my childhood, and I was also involved in track and field. I used to swim frequently, and I have always enjoyed golf.

During my time in secondary school, I liked reading books on physics, mathematics, and history. I later attended the University of Texas at Dallas to study computer science, where I served as an officer for the User Experience Club. In addition to this role, I won an internal C# programming competition with thirty participants. I also received both a Top 10 award and the Best Use of Google API Prize at a Texas A&M hackathon while I was a student there.

While at the University of Texas at Dallas, I signed a seven-year contract to work for Rockwell Collins. I subsequently worked as a software engineer in various capacities. One of the most memorable modules I took at university was Digital Logic, where I learned computer engineering theory and hardware description languages such as Verilog and VHDL. I have since applied this knowledge in both private-sector and military/government contexts across several continents.

In 2020, I had the opportunity to join the Master of Science in Data Science programme at Aberystwyth University in West Wales. During my studies, I wrote papers on algorithms, ethics, cybersecurity, databases, and deep learning. I gained practical experience working with MongoDB and Cassandra for the database module and produced a paper on convolutional neural networks for the machine learning module. The most valuable aspect of my time at Aberystwyth was studying ethics, as I believe it is essential in the modern world.
